The Guest-Book

leave a word; the next traveller will read it

By the door of every old house there is a book, and a pen on a string, and the gentle expectation that you will write something. Not a review. A trace. Proof you were here, and that for a moment we kept company. Here is ours.

Eliza M. — left on a Tuesday, in the rain

I came looking for my grandmother’s bread and found her whole kitchen. Thank you.

A passing tinker — no date given

Mended an umbrella outside. Read about clocks. Felt, for an hour, that the old trades were not so dead after all.

Thomas, age 9 — with help spelling

I LIKED THE TOY BOX BEST. THE MARBLS.

Someone far from home — late, by a borrowed light

I have not seen my country in eleven years. The room about festivals made me cry and then made me glad. Keep going.

Thank you. Your mark is made — kept, like everything here, by hand. (This being a quiet little book and not a great machine, your words stay on this page, with you. That is on purpose.)
