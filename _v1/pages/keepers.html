The Keepers of the Book

the many hands, named at last

No single person could keep a book like this, and no single person should. It was made the way a barn is raised or a quilt is pieced — by a crowd of hands, each doing the part in front of them, trusting the others to do theirs. What follows is the roll of keepers, room by room. Some wrote; some drew the margins; some only sat by the fire and said that’s not quite right, try again — which is, in its way, the most important work of all.

200 hands, gathered into 25 small companies of eight.
