The Parlour ☞ The Rooms

The Bestiary

of beasts, companions & creatures of legend

Put your hand on a horse’s neck after work, and feel what trust looks like in the body—warm, glossy, alive—and you will understand why we have never stopped keeping company with the creatures who know us.

This room holds the animals that have lived alongside us, the ones we have made into myth and the ones we have simply loved—not as nature records them, but as they live in memory and hand. A horse that remembers a path and a voice. A cat that chooses a chair in an old house. A bird at the window that teaches you to wait without moving. A hawk that comes home not from fear but from the slow, strange art of being worthy of return. These are not specimens. These are companions, wild or tame, that have left their marks on us.

In the margins of this bestiary, you will find the things we could not forget: the warmth of a creature’s breath in the dark, the grammar of command and gentleness, the particular way a robin tilts its head like a question, the monks who drew dragons not from knowledge but from longing. You will find people who kept horses and birds and the watch-fire of their own attention. You will find the small hours when silence becomes something almost like company.

Come in. These are the old stories, the patient ones, the kind that live in the body and the hand. They are here because they matter—not for what they tell us about nature, but for what they tell us about ourselves: that we have always known how to love something we cannot keep, and that this is enough.

A letter, unsigned —
I have been watching the old mare in the field behind the schoolhouse all this autumn. She stands in the rain without complaint, and when I bring her bread she takes it gentle from my palm. Today I understood: a horse remembers your hands. It remembers whether you are kind. — a reader who keeps horses
Receipt — For a bird caught in sorrow — Leave your window cracked, the sill lined with breadcrumbs and millet. Do not watch. The bird will come when your attention has turned elsewhere. Patience is the ingredient that cannot be rushed.
